Clubs

CAMS has a large variety of clubs to choose from. Here are just of a few of them.

Robotics

As CAMS does not have a football team, many students compare CAMS robotics to a usual school's football team. FRC (FIRST Robotics Competition) and VEX are the two central teams that identify CAMS.

Being guided by sponsors like Northrop Gruman, Boeing, and Raytheon, robotics inspire a generation of engineering in CAMS students.

For more information, please visit the official FRC website.

Computer Science Club

To begin with, CAMS' Computer Science Club (CSC) teaches students a variety of topics related to: cryptography, programming, virtual machines, Linux, and more. Members of CSC participate in global tournaments such as CSAW.

To find out more, please visit the official CSC website.

TED-ED Club

TED-ED club works to develop presentation literacy and public speaking skills along with animation skills to help create the next generation of problem solvers, critical thinkers and innovators to make the world a better place.

Math Club

CAMS Math Club serves the students by giving them opportunities to learn and explore mathematical topics. It is also an open forum for discussion of very interesting mathematics.

To find out more (and possibly even try some of the challenges), please visit the official CAMS Math Club page.

About CAMS

CAMS (California Academy of Mathematics and Science) is a rigorous school located in Carson, Califonia with a focus and science, mathematics, and technology (STEM). All CAMS students attend post-secondary education. CAMS was ranked 4th in the nation by Newsweek in 2014. In addition to being publicy acclaimed, students score among the top in the state in SAT testing.
